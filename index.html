1923th - 1928th

The history of Disney traces back to 1923 when Walt Disney, along with his brother Roy O. Disney, founded the Disney Brothers Cartoon Studio in Los Angeles, California. Their initial work involved producing short animated films, with their breakout success coming in 1928 with the creation of Mickey Mouse in the groundbreaking short film "Steamboat Willie."

1930th - 1937th

Throughout the 1930s and 1940s, Disney continued to innovate and release acclaimed animated features such as "Snow White and the Seven Dwarfs," the first full-length animated film in 1937, followed by classics like "Pinocchio," "Dumbo," and "Bambi.".

1955th

In the following decades, Disney expanded its entertainment empire beyond animation, venturing into television, theme parks, and live-action films. Disneyland, the first-ever Disney theme park, opened in 1955 in Anaheim, California, further solidifying Disney's place in popular culture. As the years passed, the company acquired other media companies, including Pixar Animation Studios, Marvel Entertainment, and Lucasfilm, broadening its reach and becoming a global entertainment powerhouse.
